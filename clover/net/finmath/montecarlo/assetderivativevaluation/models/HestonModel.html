19    /**
20    * This class implements a Heston Model, that is, it provides the drift and volatility specification
21    * and performs the calculation of the numeraire (consistent with the dynamics, i.e. the drift).
22    *
23    * The model is
24    * \[
25    * dS(t) = r^{\text{c}} S(t) dt + \sqrt{V(t)} S(t) dW_{1}(t), \quad S(0) = S_{0},
26    * \]
27    * \[
28    * dV(t) = \kappa ( \theta - V(t) ) dt + \xi \sqrt{V(t)} dW_{2}(t), \quad V(0) = \sigma^2,
29    * \]
30    * \[
31    * dW_{1} dW_{2} = \rho dt
32    * \]
33    * \[
34    * dN(t) = r^{\text{d}} N(t) dt, \quad N(0) = N_{0},
35    * \]
36    * where \( W \) is a Brownian motion.
37    *
38    * The class provides the model of (S,V) to an <code>{@link net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess}</code> via the specification of
39    * \( f_{1} = exp , f_{2} = identity \), \( \mu_{1} = r^{\text{c}} - \frac{1}{2} V^{+}(t) , \mu_{2} = \kappa ( \theta - V^{+}(t) ) \), \( \lambda_{1,1} = \sqrt{V^{+}(t)} , \lambda_{1,2} = 0 ,\lambda_{2,1} = \xi \sqrt{V^+(t)} \rho , \lambda_{2,2} = \xi \sqrt{V^+(t)} \sqrt{1-\rho^{2}} \), i.e.,
40    * of the SDE
41    * \[
42    * dX_{1} = \mu_{1} dt + \lambda_{1,1} dW_{1} + \lambda_{1,2} dW_{2}, \quad X_{1}(0) = \log(S_{0}),
43    * \]
44    * \[
45    * dX_{2} = \mu_{2} dt + \lambda_{2,1} dW_{1} + \lambda_{2,2} dW_{2}, \quad X_{2}(0) = V_{0} = \sigma^2,
46    * \]
47    * with \( S = f_{1}(X_{1}) , V = f_{2}(X_{2}) \).
48    * See {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess} for the notation.
49    *
50    * Here \( V^{+} \) denotes a <i>truncated</i> value of V. Different truncation schemes are available:
51    * <code>FULL_TRUNCATION</code>: \( V^{+} = max(V,0) \),
52    * <code>REFLECTION</code>: \( V^{+} = abs(V) \).
53    *
54    * The model allows to specify two independent rate for forwarding (\( r^{\text{c}} \)) and discounting (\( r^{\text{d}} \)).
55    * It thus allow for a simple modelling of a funding / collateral curve (via (\( r^{\text{d}} \)) and/or the specification of
56    * a dividend yield.
57    *
58    * The free parameters of this model are:
59    * <dl>
60    * <dt>\( S_{0} \)</dt> <dd>spot - initial value of S</dd>
61    * <dt>\( r^{\text{c}} \)</dt> <dd>the risk free rate</dd>
62    * <dt>\( \sigma \)</dt> <dd>the initial volatility level</dd>
63    * <dt>\( r^{\text{d}} \)</dt> <dd>the discount rate</dd>
64    * <dt>\( \xi \)</dt> <dd>the volatility of volatility</dd>
65    * <dt>\( \theta \)</dt> <dd>the mean reversion level of the stochastic volatility</dd>
66    * <dt>\( \kappa \)</dt> <dd>the mean reversion speed of the stochastic volatility</dd>
67    * <dt>\( \rho \)</dt> <dd>the correlation of the Brownian drivers</dd>
68    * </dl>
69    *
70    * @author Christian Fries
71    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess The interface for numerical schemes.
72    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel The interface for models provinding parameters to numerical schemes.
73    * @version 1.0
74    */

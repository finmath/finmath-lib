22    /**
23    * This class glues together a <code>MertonModel</code> and a Monte-Carlo implementation of a <code>MonteCarloProcessFromProcessModel</code>, namely <code>EulerSchemeFromProcessModel</code>,
24    * and forms a Monte-Carlo implementation of the Merton model by implementing <code>AssetModelMonteCarloSimulationModel</code>.
25    *
26    * The model is
27    * \[
28    * dS = \mu S dt + \sigma S dW + S dJ, \quad S(0) = S_{0},
29    * \]
30    * \[
31    * dN = r N dt, \quad N(0) = N_{0},
32    * \]
33    * where \( W \) is Brownian motion and \( J \) is a jump process (compound Poisson process).
34    *
35    * The process \( J \) is given by \( J(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{N(t)} (Y_{i}-1) \), where
36    * \( \log(Y_{i}) \) are i.i.d. normals with mean \( a - \frac{1}{2} b^{2} \) and standard deviation \( b \).
37    * Here \( a \) is the jump size mean and \( b \) is the jump size std. dev.
38    *
39    * For details on the construction of the model see {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.assetderivativevaluation.models.MertonModel}.
40    *
41    * @author Christian Fries
42    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.assetderivativevaluation.models.MertonModel
43    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.process.MonteCarloProcess The interface for numerical schemes.
44    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.model.ProcessModel The interface for models provinding parameters to numerical schemes.
45    * @version 1.0
46    */

28    /**
29    * This class implements an analytic swaption valuation formula under
30    * a LIBOR market model. The algorithm implemented here is the
31    * OIS discounting version of the algorithm described in
32    * ISBN 0470047224 (see {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.products.SwaptionSingleCurveAnalyticApproximation}).
33    *
34    * The approximation assumes that the forward rates (LIBOR) follow a
35    * <b>normal model</b> or <b>log-normal model</b> and that the model provides the integrated
36    * instantaneous covariance of the forward rates or the integrated
37    * instantaneous covariance of the log-forward rates
38    *
39    * The getValue method calculates the approximated integrated instantaneous variance of the swap rate,
40    * using the approximation
41    * \[
42    * \frac{d log(S(t))}{d log(L(t))} \approx \frac{d log(S(0))}{d log(L(0))} = : w.
43    * \]
44    *
45    * Since \( L \) is a vector, \( w \) is a gradient (vector). The class then approximates
46    * the Black volatility of a swaption via
47    * \[
48    * \sigma_S^{2} T := \sum_{i,j} w_{i} \gamma_{i,j} w_{j}
49    * \]
50    * where \( (\gamma_{i,j})_{i,j = 1,...,m} \) is the covariance matrix of the forward rates.
51    *
52    * The valuation can be performed in terms of value or implied Black volatility.
53    *
54    *
55    * @author Christian Fries
56    * @author Lorenzo Torricelli
57    * @date 17.05.2007.
58    * @version 1.0
59    */

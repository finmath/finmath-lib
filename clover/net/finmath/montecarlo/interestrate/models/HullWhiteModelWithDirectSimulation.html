27    /**
28    * Implements a Hull-White model with time dependent mean reversion speed and time dependent short rate volatility.
29    *
30    * <i>
31    * Note: This implementation is for illustrative purposes.
32    * For a numerically equivalent, more efficient implementation see {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.HullWhiteModel}.
33    * Please use {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.HullWhiteModel} for real applications.
34    * </i>
35    *
36    * <p>
37    * <b>Model Dynamics</b>
38    * </p>
39    *
40    * The Hull-While model assumes the following dynamic for the short rate:
41    * \[ d r(t) = ( \theta(t) - a(t) r(t) ) d t + \sigma(t) d W(t) \text{,} \quad r(t_{0}) = r_{0} \text{,} \]
42    * where the function \( \theta \) determines the calibration to the initial forward curve,
43    * \( a \) is the mean reversion and \( \sigma \) is the instantaneous volatility.
44    *
45    * The dynamic above is under the equivalent martingale measure corresponding to the numeraire
46    * \[ N(t) = \exp\left( \int_0^t r(\tau) \mathrm{d}\tau \right) \text{.} \]
47    *
48    * The main task of this class is to provide the risk-neutral drift and the volatility to the numerical scheme (given the volatility model), simulating
49    * \( r(t_{i}) \). The class then also provides and the corresponding numeraire and forward rates (LIBORs).
50    *
51    * <p>
52    * <b>Time Discrete Model</b>
53    * </p>
54    *
55    * Assuming piecewise constant coefficients (mean reversion speed \( a \) and short
56    * rate volatility \( \sigma \) the class specifies the drift and factor loadings as
57    * piecewise constant functions for an Euler-scheme.
58    * The class provides the exact Euler step for the short rate r.
59    *
60    * More specifically (assuming a constant mean reversion speed \( a \) for a moment), considering
61    * \[ \Delta \bar{r}(t_{i}) = \frac{1}{t_{i+1}-t_{i}} \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} d r(t) \]
62    * we find from
63    * \[ \exp(-a t) \ \left( \mathrm{d} \left( \exp(a t) r(t) \right) \right) \ = \ a r(t) + \mathrm{d} r(t) \ = \ \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \]
64    * that
65    * \[ \exp(a t_{i+1}) r(t_{i+1}) - \exp(a t_{i}) r(t_{i}) \ = \ \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} \left[ \exp(a t) \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \exp(a t) \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \right] \]
66    * that is
67    * \[ r(t_{i+1}) - r(t_{i}) \ = \ -(1-\exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t_{i})) r(t_{i}) + \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} \left[ \exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t)) \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t)) \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \right] \]
68    *
69    * Assuming piecewise constant \( \sigma \) and \( \theta \), being constant over \( (t_{i},t_{i}+\Delta t_{i}) \), we thus find
70    * \[ r(t_{i+1}) - r(t_{i}) \ = \ \frac{1-\exp(-a \Delta t_{i})}{a \Delta t_{i}} \left( ( \theta(t_{i}) - a \bar{r}(t_{i})) \Delta t_{i} \right) + \sqrt{\frac{1-\exp(-2 a \Delta t_{i})}{2 a \Delta t_{i}}} \sigma(t_{i}) \Delta W(t_{i}) \] .
71    *
72    * In other words, the Euler scheme is exact if the mean reversion \( a \) is replaced by the effective mean reversion
73    * \( \frac{1-\exp(-a \Delta t_{i})}{a \Delta t_{i}} a \) and the volatility is replaced by the
74    * effective volatility \( \sqrt{\frac{1-\exp(-2 a \Delta t_{i})}{2 a \Delta t_{i}}} \sigma(t_{i}) \).
75    *
76    * In the calculations above the mean reversion speed is treated as a constants, but it is straight
77    * forward to see that the same holds for piecewise constant mean reversion speeds, replacing
78    * the expression \( a \ t \) by \( \int_{0}^t a(s) \mathrm{d}s \).
79    *
80    * <p>
81    * <b>Calibration</b>
82    * </p>
83    *
84    * The drift of the short rate is calibrated to the given forward curve using
85    * \[ \theta(t) = \frac{\partial}{\partial T} f(0,t) + a(t) f(0,t) + \phi(t) \text{,} \]
86    * where the function \( f \) denotes the instantanenous forward rate and
87    * \( \phi(t) = \frac{1}{2} a \sigma^{2}(t) B(t)^{2} + \sigma^{2}(t) B(t) \frac{\partial}{\partial t} B(t) \) with \( B(t) = \frac{1-\exp(-a t)}{a} \).
88    *
89    * <p>
90    * <b>Volatility Model</b>
91    * </p>
92    *
93    * The Hull-White model is essentially equivalent to LIBOR Market Model where the forward rate <b>normal</b> volatility \( \sigma(t,T) \) is
94    * given by
95    * \[ \sigma(t,T_{i}) \ = \ (1 + L_{i}(t) (T_{i+1}-T_{i})) \sigma(t) \exp(-a (T_{i}-t)) \frac{1-\exp(-a (T_{i+1}-T_{i}))}{a (T_{i+1}-T_{i})} \]
96    * (where \( \{ T_{i} \} \) is the forward rates tenor time discretization (note that this is the <b>normal</b> volatility, not the <b>log-normal</b> volatility).
97    * Hence, we interpret both, short rate mean reversion speed and short rate volatility as part of the <i>volatility model</i>.
98    *
99    * The mean reversion speed and the short rate volatility have to be provided to this class via an object implementing
100    * {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.covariance.ShortRateVolatilityModel}.
101    *
102    *
103    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.covariance.ShortRateVolatilityModel
104    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.HullWhiteModel
105    *
106    * @author Christian Fries
107    * @version 1.2
108    */

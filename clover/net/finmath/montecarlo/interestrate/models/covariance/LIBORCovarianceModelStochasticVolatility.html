22    /**
23    * Simple stochastic volatility model, using a process
24    * \[
25    * d\lambda(t) = \nu \lambda(t) \left( \rho \mathrm{d} W_{1}(t) + \sqrt{1-\rho^{2}} \mathrm{d} W_{2}(t) \right) \text{,}
26    * \]
27    * where \( \lambda(0) = 1 \) to scale all factor loadings \( f_{i} \) returned by a given covariance model.
28    *
29    * The model constructed is \( \lambda(t) F(t) \) where \( \lambda(t) \) is
30    * the (Euler discretization of the) above process and \( F = ( f_{1}, \ldots, f_{m} ) \) is the factor loading
31    * from the given covariance model.
32    *
33    * The process uses the first two factors of the Brownian motion provided by an object implementing
34    * {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.BrownianMotion}. This can be used to generate correlations to
35    * other objects. If you like to reuse a factor of another Brownian motion use a
36    * {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.BrownianMotionView}
37    * to delegate \( ( \mathrm{d} W_{1}(t) , \mathrm{d} W_{2}(t) ) \) to a different object.
38    *
39    * The parameter of this model is a joint parameter vector, consisting
40    * of the parameter vector of the given base covariance model and
41    * appending the parameters <i>&nu;</i> and <i>&rho;</i> at the end.
42    *
43    * If this model is not calibrateable, its parameter vector is that of the
44    * covariance model, i.e., <i>&nu;</i> and <i>&rho;</i> will be not
45    * part of the calibration.
46    *
47    * For an illustration of its usage see the associated unit test.
48    *
49    * @author Christian Fries
50    * @version 1.0
51    */

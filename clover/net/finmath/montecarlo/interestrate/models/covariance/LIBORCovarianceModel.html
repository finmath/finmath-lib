25    /**
26    * Return the factor loading for a given time and a given component.
27    *
28    * The factor loading is the vector <i>f<sub>i</sub></i> such that the scalar product <br>
29    * <i>f<sub>j</sub>f<sub>k</sub> = f<sub>j,1</sub>f<sub>k,1</sub> + ... + f<sub>j,m</sub>f<sub>k,m</sub></i> <br>
30    * is the instantaneous covariance of the component <i>j</i> and <i>k</i>.
31    *
32    * With respect to simulation time <i>t</i>, this method uses a piece wise constant interpolation, i.e.,
33    * it calculates <i>t_<sub>i</sub></i> such that <i>t_<sub>i</sub></i> is the largest point in <code>getTimeDiscretization</code>
34    * such that <i>t_<sub>i</sub> &le; t </i>.
35    *
36    * The component here, it given via a double <i>T</i> which may be associated with the LIBOR fixing date.
37    * With respect to component time <i>T</i>, this method uses a piece wise constant interpolation, i.e.,
38    * it calculates <i>T_<sub>j</sub></i> such that <i>T_<sub>j</sub></i> is the largest point in <code>getTimeDiscretization</code>
39    * such that <i>T_<sub>j</sub> &le; T </i>.
40    *
41    * @param time The time <i>t</i> at which factor loading is requested.
42    * @param component The component time (as a double associated with the fixing of the forward rate) <i>T<sub>i</sub></i>.

28    /**
29    * Implements a Hull-White model with time dependent mean reversion speed and time dependent short rate volatility.
30    *
31    * <i>
32    * Note: This implementation is for illustrative purposes.
33    * For a numerically equivalent, more efficient implementation see {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.HullWhiteModel}.
34    * Please use {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.HullWhiteModel} for real applications.
35    * </i>
36    *
37    * <p>
38    * <b>Model Dynamics</b>
39    * </p>
40    *
41    * The Hull-While model assumes the following dynamic for the short rate:
42    * \[ d r(t) = ( \theta(t) - a(t) r(t) ) d t + \sigma(t) d W(t) \text{,} \quad r(t_{0}) = r_{0} \text{,} \]
43    * where the function \( \theta \) determines the calibration to the initial forward curve,
44    * \( a \) is the mean reversion and \( \sigma \) is the instantaneous volatility.
45    *
46    * The dynamic above is under the equivalent martingale measure corresponding to the numeraire
47    * \[ N(t) = \exp\left( \int_0^t r(\tau) \mathrm{d}\tau \right) \text{.} \]
48    *
49    * The main task of this class is to provide the risk-neutral drift and the volatility to the numerical scheme (given the volatility model), simulating
50    * \( r(t_{i}) \). The class then also provides and the corresponding numeraire and forward rates (LIBORs).
51    *
52    * <p>
53    * <b>Time Discrete Model</b>
54    * </p>
55    *
56    * Assuming piecewise constant coefficients (mean reversion speed \( a \) and short
57    * rate volatility \( \sigma \) the class specifies the drift and factor loadings as
58    * piecewise constant functions for an Euler-scheme.
59    * The class provides the exact Euler step for the short rate r.
60    *
61    * More specifically (assuming a constant mean reversion speed \( a \) for a moment), considering
62    * \[ \Delta \bar{r}(t_{i}) = \frac{1}{t_{i+1}-t_{i}} \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} d r(t) \]
63    * we find from
64    * \[ \exp(-a t) \ \left( \mathrm{d} \left( \exp(a t) r(t) \right) \right) \ = \ a r(t) + \mathrm{d} r(t) \ = \ \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \]
65    * that
66    * \[ \exp(a t_{i+1}) r(t_{i+1}) - \exp(a t_{i}) r(t_{i}) \ = \ \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} \left[ \exp(a t) \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \exp(a t) \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \right] \]
67    * that is
68    * \[ r(t_{i+1}) - r(t_{i}) \ = \ -(1-\exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t_{i})) r(t_{i}) + \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{i+1}} \left[ \exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t)) \theta(t) \mathrm{d}t + \exp(-a (t_{i+1}-t)) \sigma(t) \mathrm{d}W(t) \right] \]
69    *
70    * Assuming piecewise constant \( \sigma \) and \( \theta \), being constant over \( (t_{i},t_{i}+\Delta t_{i}) \), we thus find
71    * \[ r(t_{i+1}) - r(t_{i}) \ = \ \frac{1-\exp(-a \Delta t_{i})}{a \Delta t_{i}} \left( ( \theta(t_{i}) - a \bar{r}(t_{i})) \Delta t_{i} \right) + \sqrt{\frac{1-\exp(-2 a \Delta t_{i})}{2 a \Delta t_{i}}} \sigma(t_{i}) \Delta W(t_{i}) \] .
72    *
73    * In other words, the Euler scheme is exact if the mean reversion \( a \) is replaced by the effective mean reversion
74    * \( \frac{1-\exp(-a \Delta t_{i})}{a \Delta t_{i}} a \) and the volatility is replaced by the
75    * effective volatility \( \sqrt{\frac{1-\exp(-2 a \Delta t_{i})}{2 a \Delta t_{i}}} \sigma(t_{i}) \).
76    *
77    * In the calculations above the mean reversion speed is treated as a constants, but it is straight
78    * forward to see that the same holds for piecewise constant mean reversion speeds, replacing
79    * the expression \( a \ t \) by \( \int_{0}^t a(s) \mathrm{d}s \).
80    *
81    * <p>
82    * <b>Calibration</b>
83    * </p>
84    *
85    * The drift of the short rate is calibrated to the given forward curve using
86    * \[ \theta(t) = \frac{\partial}{\partial T} f(0,t) + a(t) f(0,t) + \phi(t) \text{,} \]
87    * where the function \( f \) denotes the instantanenous forward rate and
88    * \( \phi(t) = \frac{1}{2} a \sigma^{2}(t) B(t)^{2} + \sigma^{2}(t) B(t) \frac{\partial}{\partial t} B(t) \) with \( B(t) = \frac{1-\exp(-a t)}{a} \).
89    *
90    * <p>
91    * <b>Volatility Model</b>
92    * </p>
93    *
94    * The Hull-White model is essentially equivalent to LIBOR Market Model where the forward rate <b>normal</b> volatility \( \sigma(t,T) \) is
95    * given by
96    * \[ \sigma(t,T_{i}) \ = \ (1 + L_{i}(t) (T_{i+1}-T_{i})) \sigma(t) \exp(-a (T_{i}-t)) \frac{1-\exp(-a (T_{i+1}-T_{i}))}{a (T_{i+1}-T_{i})} \]
97    * (where \( \{ T_{i} \} \) is the forward rates tenor time discretization (note that this is the <b>normal</b> volatility, not the <b>log-normal</b> volatility).
98    * Hence, we interpret both, short rate mean reversion speed and short rate volatility as part of the <i>volatility model</i>.
99    *
100    * The mean reversion speed and the short rate volatility have to be provided to this class via an object implementing
101    * {@link net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.covariance.ShortRateVolatilityModel}.
102    *
103    *
104    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.covariance.ShortRateVolatilityModel
105    * @see net.finmath.montecarlo.interestrate.models.HullWhiteModel
106    *
107    * @author Christian Fries
108    * @version 1.2
109    */

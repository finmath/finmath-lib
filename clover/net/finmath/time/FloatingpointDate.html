16    /**
17    * This class provides the library wide conversion from a floating point number to a LocalDate.
18    *
19    * While in practical applications a date or time should always be represented with a proper
20    * date class, e.g. <code>LocalDate</code> or <code>LocalDateTime</code>. In financial applications the measurement
21    * of time distances has to be based on a solid definition, e.g., daycount conventions to calculate
22    * daycount fractions.
23    *
24    * However, many mathematical models described in text books rely on time being modeled as some
25    * real value \( t \).
26    *
27    * To allow for both approaches to co-exists this class fixes the interpretation of a floating
28    * point number representing time, <i>unless otherwise specified</i>.
29    *
30    * So it still possible that models use their own "conversion".
31    *
32    * Examples where the specification of this contract is important:
33    * - the way of measuring time in an NSS curve determines the interpretation of the NSS parameters.
34    * - in the textbook Black-Scholes models, multiplying volatility by W(t), changing from an ACT/365 to ACT/360 would represent a re-scaling of the volatilities.
35    *
36    * The specification of this internal time representation is to some extend just a convention which defines the unit of internal model parameters.
37    * A constant volatility measured against a floating point time ACT/365 corresponds 1:1: to a constant volatility measure again a floating point time ACT/360
38    * with a conversion factor of 360/365. However, the situation is not always trivial, since a constant volatility measured against a floating point time ACT/365
39    * would correspond to a weakly time-dependent volatility volatility measured against ACT/ACT.
40    *
41    * Such nuances represent differences in the model, a model calibration will ensure that the parameters are choosen such that the model matches observed quantities
42    * independent from the convention used to express internal model parameters.
43    *
44    * @author Christian Fries
45    * @version 1.1
46    */

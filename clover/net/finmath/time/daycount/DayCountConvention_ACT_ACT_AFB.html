13    /**
14    * Implementation of ACT/ACT AFB.
15    *
16    * Calculates the day count by calculating the actual number of days between startDate and endDate.
17    *
18    * A fractional day is rounded to the approximately nearest day.
19    *
20    * The day count fraction is calculated using ACT_ACT_AFB convention. The algorithm works as follows:
21    * <ul>
22    * <li>
23    * If the interval from startDate to endDate spans more than a whole year, then
24    * the number of whole years is subtracted from the endDate year and added (as an integer) to the day count fraction.
25    * Here, subtraction of whole year(s) means:
26    * <ul>
27    * <li>
28    * If the end date is on a February 29th and the resulting day is not in a leap year,
29    * it will be set to February 28th of that year (i.e., we preserve "end of month").
30    * </li>
31    * <li>
32    * If the end date is on a February 28th of a non-leap year and the resulting day is in a leap year,
33    * it will be on February 29th of that year (i.e., we preserve "end of month").
34    * </li>
35    * <li>
36    * Otherwise the resulting day has the same day of month and the same month as the end year.
37    * </li>
38    * </ul>
39    * </li>
40    * <li>
41    * For the remaining interval the actual number of days is divided by a denominator,
42    * where the denominator is 366.0 if February 29th is in between start and end and 365.0 otherwise.
43    * </li>
44    * </ul>
45    *
46    * @author Christian Fries
47    * @version 1.0
48    */
